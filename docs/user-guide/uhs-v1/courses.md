# Courses Guide

Urban Home School offers a wide range of courses aligned with Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC). This guide explains how to browse, enroll in, complete, and get the most out of your courses.

---

## Browsing the Course Catalog

### Accessing the Catalog

1. Click **Courses** in the sidebar.
2. The course catalog displays all available courses, showing the title, thumbnail, instructor name, rating, and price.

### Searching for Courses

Use the **search bar** at the top of the catalog to find courses by:

- Subject name (e.g., "Mathematics" or "Kiswahili").
- Topic (e.g., "fractions" or "photosynthesis").
- Instructor name.
- Keywords related to what you want to learn.

## Filtering by Grade Level

You can filter courses by grade to see only the content relevant to you.

1. In the course catalog, look for the **Grade** filter.
2. Select your grade level (Grade 1 through Grade 12).
3. The catalog will refresh to show courses designed for that grade.
4. You can also select multiple grades if you want to browse content slightly above or below your level.

The grade levels follow the CBC structure:

| Stage | Grades |
|---|---|
| Lower Primary | Grade 1 to Grade 3 |
| Upper Primary | Grade 4 to Grade 6 |
| Junior Secondary | Grade 7 to Grade 9 |
| Senior Secondary | Grade 10 to Grade 12 |

## Course Detail Page

Click on any course from the catalog to open its detail page. Here is what you will find:

### Overview

- **Course title**: The name of the course.
- **Description**: A summary of what you will learn and who the course is for.
- **Instructor**: The teacher who created the course, with a link to their profile.
- **Rating**: The average star rating from student reviews.
- **Number of enrollments**: How many students have enrolled.
- **Last updated**: When the course was most recently modified.

### Syllabus

The syllabus lists every module and lesson in the course:

- **Modules**: Major sections or units.
- **Lessons**: Individual topics within each module.
- **Assessments**: Quizzes, assignments, or projects attached to the course.
- **Estimated time**: How long each lesson or module takes to complete.

This gives you a complete picture of the course content before you enroll.

### Instructor Information

- The instructor's name, photo, and bio.
- Other courses they teach.
- Their average rating and total students.

### Reviews

- Star ratings (1 to 5) from students who have taken the course.
- Written reviews with feedback about the content, teaching quality, and overall experience.
- You can sort reviews by most recent, highest rated, or lowest rated.

## Enrolling in Free and Paid Courses

### Free Courses

1. Click **Enroll for Free** on the course detail page.
2. You are immediately added to the course.
3. The course appears in **My Courses** in the sidebar.

### Paid Courses

1. Click **Enroll** on the course detail page.
2. The price is displayed in KES (Kenyan Shillings).
3. Choose your payment method:
   - **M-Pesa**: Enter your phone number, receive a prompt, enter your PIN.
   - **PayPal**: Complete payment through PayPal.
   - **Card**: Enter your credit or debit card details.
   - **Wallet**: Pay from your Urban Home School wallet balance.
4. Confirm the payment.
5. Once payment is confirmed, you are enrolled and the course appears in **My Courses**.

### Enrollment Confirmation

After enrolling, you will receive:

- An in-app notification confirming your enrollment.
- An email with course details and a direct link to start.
- A receipt for paid courses.

## Taking Lessons and Tracking Progress

### Starting a Course

1. Go to **My Courses** in the sidebar.
2. Click on the course you want to work on.
3. The course page shows your progress and the next lesson to complete.
4. Click on the next lesson to begin.

### Lesson Structure

Each lesson typically includes:

- **Content**: Text explanations, images, diagrams, or embedded videos.
- **Examples**: Worked examples that show how to apply what you are learning.
- **Practice activities**: Short exercises within the lesson.
- **Resources**: Downloadable files such as PDFs or worksheets.

### Completing a Lesson

1. Read through or watch all the lesson content.
2. Complete any in-lesson activities.
3. Click **Mark as Complete** at the bottom of the lesson.
4. The next lesson in the sequence unlocks automatically.

### Tracking Your Progress

- A **progress bar** on the course page shows how much of the course you have finished.
- Each completed lesson is marked with a checkmark.
- Your dashboard shows a summary of progress across all your courses.
- The Bird AI may remind you to continue courses you have not worked on recently.

## Completing Courses and Earning Certificates

### What Counts as Completion

A course is considered complete when you have:

- Finished all lessons in every module.
- Completed all required assessments (quizzes, assignments, projects, or exams).
- Met the minimum passing score on required assessments.

### Earning a Certificate

Once you complete a course:

1. A certificate is automatically generated.
2. You will see a congratulations message with a link to view your certificate.
3. The certificate includes your name, the course title, the instructor's name, and the completion date.
4. You can download, print, or share your certificate.

For more details on certificates, see the Certificates Guide.

## Rating and Reviewing Courses

After completing a course (or making significant progress), you can leave a review to help other students.

### How to Leave a Review

1. Go to the course page.
2. Scroll to the **Reviews** section.
3. Click **Write a Review**.
4. Select a star rating (1 to 5 stars):
   - 1 star: Poor
   - 2 stars: Below average
   - 3 stars: Average
   - 4 stars: Good
   - 5 stars: Excellent
5. Write your review. Mention what you liked, what could be improved, and who the course is best suited for.
6. Click **Submit Review**.

### Review Guidelines

- Be honest and constructive.
- Focus on the course content and teaching quality.
- Avoid personal comments about the instructor.
- Do not include spoilers about quiz or exam questions.

Your review helps other students decide whether the course is right for them and helps instructors improve their content.
